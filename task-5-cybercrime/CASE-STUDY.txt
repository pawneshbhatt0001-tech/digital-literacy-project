#PHISHING


Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

In a typical phishing attack, a student may receive an email claiming to be from a bank or an online platform. The message may say that their account has a problem and ask them to click on a link. When the student clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website that looks real. If they enter their login details, the attacker can steal the information and misuse it.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not recognize fake messages easily. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and loss of personal data.

Therefore, it is important to be careful while clicking on links and always verify the source before sharing any sensitive information.
